Dr. Kwame Nkrumah was a Ghanaian politician and statesman who played a key role in the country's struggle
for independence. He was born on September 21, 1909, in Nkroful, Gold Coast (now Ghana), and died on April
27, 1972, in Bucharest, Romania. Nkrumah was a leading figure in the African independence movement and the
first president of Ghana. He was a strong advocate for Pan-Africanism and African unity, and he played a key role
in the formation of the Organization of African Unity (OAU), which is now the African Union (AU).
Here is a brief overview of his life and legacy:
Early Life and Education:
- Nkrumah was born in Nkroful, a small village in the Gold Coast (now Ghana)
- He attended Achimota School in Ghana and later studied at Lincoln University
in Pennsylvania, USA
Political Career:
- Nkrumah returned to Ghana in 1947 and became involved in politics
- He founded the Convention People's Party (CPP) and led the party to victory in the 1951 general elections
- He became the first prime minister of Ghana in 1951 and led the country to independence in 1957
- He was elected president of Ghana in 1960 and served until he was overthrown in a military coup in 1966
Legacy:
- Nkrumah was a strong advocate for African unity and Pan-Africanism
- He played a key role in the formation of the OAU (now AU) and was a leading figure in the African independence
movement
- He is remembered as a champion of African liberation and a pioneer of African unity
- His legacy continues to inspire Africans and people of African descent around the world
